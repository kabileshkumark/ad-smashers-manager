## Release Summary

Version 1.0 remains the formal release line. Technical build 1.0.8 stabilizes the complete payment cycle and extends activities to support multiple payers and four explicit split methods without changing the Firebase project or Firestore security rules.

Advance and Credit remain owned by the player who provided the funds. A saved payment group may use that payer's available funds for active group-member dues, but ownership is never pooled or transferred. Activities now compare each non-organizer player's allocated share with that player's contribution: a positive balance is Due to the configured Organizer, while an over-contribution is Credit owed back to that player.

## Included Changes

- Payment-group payer Advance and Credit cover the payer's own collectible dues first, then eligible saved-group member dues in deterministic order.
- Funding is reserved once across groups and remains attributable to its owner in history.
- Eligible user-entered receipts offer explicit Delete or Reverse choices. Reverse preserves an audit row; Delete removes the row after undoing the same allocations.
- Activities accept one or more payer contributions whose total must equal the activity total.
- Activity splits support Equal, Manual amount, Percentage, and No. of Shares modes with exact-cent allocation.
- The Organizer selected in Settings is snapshotted as the settlement owner. Non-organizer balances become organizer-relative Due or Credit.
- Editing or deleting an activity reconciles its derived balances and existing receipts instead of blocking all financial fields.
- Activity-generated Credit uses the canonical Credit ledger and can cover the owner's later personal or saved-group dues.
- Player Balances show non-zero Due, Advance, or Credit, use `Settled` for covered amounts, and apply the agreed deterministic ordering.
- The authenticated app render reuses one canonical ledger snapshot to remove the interaction slowdown introduced during payment-cycle work.
- Payment and activity controls remain in one responsive row at 320 px, 390 px, and desktop widths.
